Marian Apparitions

Since the death, resurrection, and transfiguration of Christ, physical manifestations of Mary have appeared to tens of thousands of people. In recent centuries, such apparitions have become increasingly common, due in part to greater interconnectedness and access to information. Scholars estimate that 2,500 independent Marian Apparitions have occurred throughout history, 308 of which have been attributed to saints or other holy individuals. 7 Popes have, themselves, witnessed a Marian apparition.

Our Lady of the Pillar

January 2, 40 AD

Zaragoza, Spain

According to tradition, the Apostle James was preaching in Roman Hispania, praying on the Ebro river, when Mary appeared on a nearby pillar, accompanied by thousands of Angels. This is the only recorded instance of the bilocation of Mary, whereby she was alive in Jerusalem at the time of the apparition. The feast day of this apparition coincides with the discovery of America, prompting Mary to receive the title of 'Patroness of the Hispanic World.' This apparition is the very first to occur in Christian history, marking a turning point, and emphasizing Mary's support of the missionary efforts of the Apostles.

Our Lady of Le Puy

~250 AD

A widow named Villa, who was a new convert, became very sick with a high fever. Villa prayed to the Virgin Mary, who appeared to her, telling her to climb Mount Anis to be healed. Villa had her servant carry her to the place indicated, where she was placed on a nearby rock. Villa fell asleep, experiencing a dream in which an angel told her that Mary had chosen that very rock to be invoked upon. Villa was completely healed, and rushed to tell the bishop, Saint George of Velay. A fence was built around the rock, and later it became the location of a new church alter. The apparition represents Mary's unceasing care for the sick and lowly.
